Let's do a quick study of two similar cases.

The folllowing one is an original Jack Justis case made in 2006 according to the Justis signature on the bottom.  The owner of  the case is a very good player from Taiwan who graciously lent it to me for the purpose of this pictorial.

Original Price:

This is the case in it's original state and after two years of use.















This is the "ProLite" interior that is made at the Long Chan Cue Case factory in China.  Notice that it doesn't fit properly.  I speculate that the reason it fits loosely is because the case maker did not want to change his pattern to fit this size of tube.



This interior is the exact same as that found on many models of inexpensive cases that are also made by the Long Chan Case factory.  In fact, although the case maker has named it "ProLite" and claims to have invented it in fact, fabric liners inside of plastic shells for cue cases as been around since the 70's at least.  Again notice the ill fit.



Here it is shown that the interior tube is easily removable.  The maker claims that this is a "feature".  We think it's an excuse to cover up a poor fit.





This is the bottom of the liner as it came out of the case.  Notice that there is no padding whatsoever nor is the tube sealed in any way.
